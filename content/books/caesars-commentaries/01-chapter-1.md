{{< bp >}}
{{< name "Julius Caesar" "JOO-lee-us SEE-zer" >}} begins his story by describing {{< name "Gaul" "GAWL" >}}, the large land where many of these events take place. He says that all Gaul is divided into three main parts.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
One part belongs to the {{< name "Belgae" "BEL-guy" >}}. Another belongs to the {{< name "Aquitani" "ak-wih-TAH-nee" >}}. The third belongs to the people who call themselves {{< name "Celts" "KELTS" >}}, but whom the Romans call {{< name "Gauls" "GAWLZ" >}}.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
These peoples are not all the same. Caesar says they have different languages, customs, and laws. Rivers mark many of their borders. The {{< name "Garonne" "ga-RON" >}} River separates the Gauls from the Aquitani. The {{< name "Marne" "MARN" >}} and the {{< name "Seine" "SEN" >}} separate the Gauls from the Belgae.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
Caesar says the Belgae are the bravest of these peoples. That is his Roman point of view, and he gives his reasons. The Belgae live farthest from the Roman Province, where Roman ways and Roman comforts are common. Merchants do not often travel to them with luxuries. Caesar believed such luxuries could make people softer and less ready for war.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
The Belgae also live near the {{< name "Germans" "JER-munz" >}}, who dwell beyond the {{< name "Rhine" "RYN" >}} River. They are often at war with them. For the same reason, Caesar says the {{< name "Helvetii" "hel-VEE-shee-eye" >}} are also very brave. They fight the Germans almost every day, either driving them away from Helvetian land or carrying war to the German border.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
Caesar then describes the shape of the land. The part occupied by the Gauls begins at the {{< name "Rhone" "ROHN" >}} River. It is bounded by the Garonne, the ocean, and the lands of the Belgae. On the side near the {{< name "Sequani" "seh-KWAH-nee" >}} and the Helvetii, it reaches toward the Rhine and stretches northward.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
The Belgae live toward the far edge of Gaul. Their land runs down toward the lower Rhine and faces north and east. {{< name "Aquitania" "ak-wih-TAY-nee-uh" >}} reaches from the Garonne to the {{< name "Pyrenees" "PEER-uh-neez" >}} mountains and to the part of the ocean near Spain.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
This opening may feel like a map lesson, but it matters. Caesar wants the reader to understand where each people lives, who their neighbors are, and why some tribes are used to danger and war.
{{< /bp >}}

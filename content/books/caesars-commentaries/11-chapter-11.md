{{< bp >}}
The next day, Caesar had to think again about food. Only two days remained before he needed to give grain to his army.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
He was not more than eighteen miles from {{< name "Bibracte" "bee-BRAK-teh" >}}, the largest and best-supplied town of the {{< name "Aedui" "EYE-dyoo-eye" >}}. Caesar decided he must secure grain for his soldiers.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
So he turned away from the {{< name "Helvetii" "hel-VEE-shee-eye" >}} and began marching quickly toward Bibracte.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
Some deserters from {{< name "Lucius Aemilius" "LOO-shus eye-MIL-ee-us" >}}, a captain of the Gallic cavalry, told the Helvetii what Caesar was doing.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
The Helvetii changed their plan. Perhaps they thought the Romans were afraid and retreating, especially because Caesar had not fought the day before even after taking the higher ground. Or perhaps they hoped to cut the Romans off from their food supply.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
Whatever their reason, they turned from their road and began to follow Caesar's army. They attacked and troubled the Romans from the rear.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
When Caesar saw this, he drew his forces onto a nearby hill. He sent the cavalry to hold back the enemy's attack.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
Meanwhile, Caesar arranged his four veteran legions halfway up the hill in three lines. Veteran soldiers were experienced soldiers who had already served in war.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
Above them, on the very top of the hill, Caesar placed the two newer legions he had recently raised in Hither Gaul, along with all the auxiliary troops. Auxiliaries were supporting troops who helped the main Roman legions.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
Caesar ordered the whole mountain to be covered with men. He also ordered the baggage to be gathered into one place and guarded by the soldiers posted higher up the hill.
{{< /bp >}}

{{< bp >}}
The Helvetii came on with all their wagons. They gathered their baggage into one place too. Then, after driving back Caesar's cavalry, they formed a phalanx and advanced in close order against the Roman front line. A phalanx was a tight battle formation in which men stood close together with shields and weapons.
{{< /bp >}}
